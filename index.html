Objects you keeping safe during a pandemic

Welcome to my assignment. Today we’ll be talking about objects that can keep you safe during a pandemic, and how I kept myself safe during the COVID-19 pandemic.

PART 1 — 
Alchool-based hand sanitisers
Viruses live on surfaces
1

Working at a warehouse during the pandemic, there were literally thousands of ‘high-touch areas’.


Hence, it was important to frequently use alcohol-based hand sanitisers as there was a very high probability that COVID lived on at least one surface I worked with.

2

At the start of the COVID-19 pandemic, mask-wearing was made a requirement to prevent the spread of the virus.


However, it wasn’t the only precaution you needed to take to stay safe during the pandemic.

3

Viruses can live on surfaces for quite a while, so it’s important that you use hand sanitisers (specifically with 70% alcohol concentration) frequently.

PART 2 — 
EFFECTIVE MASK-WEARING
Wear your mask correctly
Masks will only protect you if you pick and wear them correctly. Cloth masks are a popular option during a pandemic, as there will always typically be a price surge for medical-grade masks due to increased demand. Wearing a cloth mask is better than nothing, though.
Make sure your mask covers your mouth and nose. If your nose is exposed, you are essentially breathing unprotected air and therefore increasing your odds of becoming infected.
PART 3 — 
IDENTIFICATION CARDS
Be prepared to identify yourself

You may want to carry additional cards such as your bank or medicare card in case authorities doubt the authenticity of your identification card.

Official identity cards like drivers licenses, proof of age cards, and passports are all valid forms of identification in a pandemic.

Having proof of identity is important during a pandemic. Oftentimes, as it was during COVID-19, strict government restrictions will be put in place.

Police will have increased powers to stop the spread of the virus, which includes stopping anyone in public to ensure no restrictions are being broken.

Having proof of identity means any encounters with the police go smooth, which will put your mind at ease. Living during a pandemic is stressful enough, and you don’t want to add any additional stresses at these times.
PART 4 — 
SMARTPHONES
Your smartphone is a tool
Your smartphone plays an important role in that you can use it to help you feel calm in stressful situations, be it listening to music or playing a game.
Your smartphone can be used as a means of escape from the realities of living during a pandemic. When you’re out in public, it can take a toll on you to constantly remind yourself that everyone around you could infect you at any time.
PART 5 — 
GLOVES
Glove wearing
Contrary to popular belief, wearing gloves during a pandemic only offer a low level of protection.
misconceptions
Most diseases don’t infect you by touch -- they must enter into one of your body cavities some way or another.

Gloves are most effective when they are treated like masks (meaning you must think they are dirty all of the time) combined with proper hand hygiene.
psychological protection
Wearing gloves offer you better peace of mind as diseases will never physically touch your body unless you let them.

This is useful for working in stressful environments where you forget there even is a pandemic at all.
PART 6 — 
Protective eye glasses
Protect your eyes
Wearing protective eyeglasses is useful if you know you will be in an area where people will not obey mask mandates or a high risk infection area.

Though you do not necessarily need to wear protective eyeglasses to get a similar level of protection (normal glasses will work too).
Your eyes are the only part of your body completely exposed when out in public. Protective eyeglasses are the ideal way to prevent any droplets from potentially infecting you.
PART 7 — 
Wireless earphones
Keep yourself occupied
!
Wearing wireless earphones is not exactly a measure you can take to keep yourself from becoming infected, but rather a strategy to manage your stress levels in public. When you’re in a location where you have to be in close proximity to someone who could be infected, it’s counter-intuitive to give all your attention to them.
I’ve found that being stressed from being near potentially infectious people isn’t good, as it can take a toll on your overall energy and mental health.
PART 8 — 
Your own pens
Use your own writing material

Do not share your stationery

Under no circumstances should you ever share or borrow pens during a pandemic. When people sneeze or cough, viral residue can get on their hands, which gets on stationery when they touch them.

Not to mention people chew on their stationery sometimes too!
Using your own pen means that you won’t have to bear the risk of touching a contaminated object. As a result, you won’t have to wash your hands after touching your pen.
It’s good practice to never use shared pens anyways, as they are almost never cleaned.
